Know Before You Go

Rio Travel Tips and Requirements

YES — The $2,000 Bank Requirement Is Real

As of April 10, 2025, US citizens must obtain a Brazil eVisa AND show proof of at least $2,000 in their bank account as part of the application. Submit 2 consecutive months of bank statements showing this balance — or 6 months of pay stubs. The eVisa costs $80.90 and is valid for 10 years. Apply at the official Brazilian Embassy/VFS portal at least 4–6 weeks before travel. Do NOT use third-party scam sites. For a bougie 6-day trip you'll easily have this — just make sure your statements are current, clearly show your name, and the balance is visible.

eVisa — Required for US Citizens

Apply online at the official Brazilian eVisa portal (vfsglobal.com). Fee: $80.90. Valid 10 years, multi-entry, up to 90 days per stay. Apply 4–6 weeks before departure minimum.

What to Bring

Valid US passport (6+ months past Feb 10, 2027), eVisa printout, hotel/villa confirmation, return flight proof, travel insurance docs, and prescription meds with prescriptions.

Health Requirements

Yellow fever vaccine strongly recommended (required if arriving from certain countries). Also: Hepatitis A and B, Typhoid. Visit a travel health clinic 4–6 weeks before departure.

Money and Cards

Brazilian Real (BRL). $1 USD = approx 5–6 BRL. Use a Wise card or Charles Schwab debit — zero foreign transaction fees. Tell your bank before you travel. Carry some cash Reais for Lapa street bars and beach vendors.

→Use Uber or private driver at night — never random street taxis
→99% of tourists have zero issues — just don't be careless

Cultural Notes for Black Travelers in Rio

Rio has a large and vibrant Afro-Brazilian community, and its samba and Carnival culture literally originates from African heritage brought during the diaspora. You may feel a deep cultural connection in the music, food, and spirit of the city. Engage authentically with Afro-Brazilian neighborhoods like Madureira, samba schools, and Candomblé traditions. This is your culture's diaspora too — experience it fully.
